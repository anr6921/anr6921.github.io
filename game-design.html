Hello, World!

Hello World is an open source game focused on teaching the basics of object oriented code. In a desolate factory, you play as ACE, a lone robot distraught from all the programming bugs messing up his system! It is up to the player to open ACE's console and re-program his left and right movement, jump, and collecting abilities.

Created in 24 hours for the 2017 WiC Hackathon, Hello World won two awards: Best Teaching Programming Hack sponsored by Hubspot, and Best Open Source Hack sponsored by Red Hat.

My Contributions

I contributed to the development of Hello World as the lead Game Designer and Architect. I coded all of the background proccessing used in the WinForm, or "terminal". This meant providing a hint for user input, checking to see if user input was acceptable (and if it wasn't, send an error message), and finally, implimenting the WinForm as part of our Monogame project. In addition to the WinForm, I also programmed the animation for player spritesheets, set up the classes, constructors and attributes.

Unmasked

Unmasked is a sidescrolling platform game that has various mazes for the player to solve. The game tells a story of a lone lumberjack living in a nondescript forest, who one day discovers a mask that warps the viewpoint of his world. When he dons the mask, a chilling and twisted world is revealed, existing simultaneously and separately to his own. Trying to destroy the monsters in this new world and figure out what exactly is going on, our protagonist must switch between his old world and this new one to traverse the environments and make sense of his newfound dual-existence.

As the designer, I was in charge of communicating and implimenting each individual's vision into one collective idea. Additionally, I helped with numerous tasks outside my designated position, assisting my fellow teammates in areas such as player movement, animation code, player attack, and asset production.

Animation

I created the animation method so it could be easily used by all moveable game entities, and it was very easy to implement whenever we added a new spritesheet.

In addition to code, I also drew all of the lumberjack's animation using Adobe Photoshop.

Gloombox

Gloombox is a 2D platformer created in Unity. It is currently being produced by a graduate student start up, Pentatonic Games, in Rochester, New York. My contribution to this project is part of an independant study to provide both conceptual and refined art assests to the Gloombox team.

NPC Creation

One of my main contributions to Gloombox has been the production of NPCs, or "Clefs". Using photoshop, I created animated sprite sheets for each of the Clef's emotive abilities, which are triggered by player interaction.
